Music Setup

Two things stay wired and never move: the L8 is the permanent audio hub (always hear it, always record stems) and the Audio4c is the permanent MIDI matrix (it picks who's the clock master and keeps working with the laptop off). Changing modes = recalling a stored Audio4c scene or moving one patch cable — never rewiring the rig.

The patchbay is shown from the rear (24 on the left, 1 on the right). Each column has two jacks: the top jack is where a device output plugs in, the bottom jack patches out to a device input. The letter between a column's two jacks is its norm type: H half-normalled · T thru · N normalled. Audio cables: solid = balanced (TRS or XLR), dashed = unbalanced (TS or RCA), thick = speaker cable.

Three ways to feed the Octatrack

1 One synth OT Mult it at the patchbay so it feeds the L8 (record) and the OT (sample) at once. Clean, free.
2 Live OT A Live output bus Audio4c out 1/2 patchbay OT (half-normalled, so it's always there).
No-aggregate fallback: Live L8 USB return an L8 monitor send OT.		 Needs an Aggregate Device — see Setup.
3 A blend OT Patch several taps into an external submixer (add one back — this is the job the Yamaha did), its main out OT. Custom submix; keeps the L8's monitor mixes free.

The two sessions

Jam & Capture

Play, hit record, each synth lands as its own stem on the L8's SD card — laptop optional. Brain is whichever Audio4c scene you're in (Live, or Octatrack with the lid closed).

Compose

Drag the SD stems into Live, arrange, overdub synths live (they're already on L8 channels already in Live over USB). Octatrack there as a drum machine if you want it, ignored if not.

Sampling Live into the Octatrack

MIDI
An OT MIDI track plays a Live instrument (matrix routes OT Mac).
Audio
Live's output rides a Live bus Audio4c out OT inputs, and the OT samples it.

The laptop becomes another voice you can sequence and sample in the box.

Setup notes

USB hub
The M2 Air has 2 USB-C ports total. Put the L8, Audio4c, and Push on a powered hub so the bus-powered gear stays stable. The keyboard goes into the Audio4c's USB host port, so it still plays synths with the laptop off.
Live OT, the clean way
In macOS, make an Aggregate Device = L8 + Audio4c, with the L8 as clock master. In Live, inputs come from the L8; assign one output pair to Audio4c OUT 1/2 = your "send to OT" bus (half-normalled to the OT at cols 1–2). Check for drift the first session; if you hear it, the L8-monitor fallback avoids aggregation entirely.
Monitoring stays on the L8
Zero-latency analog. Turn Live's monitoring off so you don't double-hear with USB latency.
